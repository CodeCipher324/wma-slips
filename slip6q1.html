Why Diwali is Celebrated

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most significant festivals in India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. The festival is celebrated by lighting diyas, bursting crackers, and sharing sweets with loved ones.

It marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana, as well as the worship of Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity and happiness.
